# Instructor Overview

## Course positioning

These materials can be used in an introductory applied psychology statistics or research methods course. The focus is not advanced programming, but practical, transparent, and reproducible analysis.

## Main teaching idea

Students learn R through a realistic research workflow:

1. understanding the project folder;
2. importing data;
3. checking variable types;
4. dealing with missing values;
5. scoring scales;
6. checking reliability;
7. describing data;
8. visualizing data;
9. testing simple hypotheses;
10. reporting results.

## Suggested learning activities

- Live coding demonstration
- Pair work
- Short interpretation tasks
- Mini-report writing
- Reproducibility checklist
- Comparison between SPSS-style output and R output

## Suggested assessment

Students can submit:

1. an annotated R script;
2. one exported table;
3. one exported figure;
4. a short interpretation of one statistical result;
5. a reflection on reproducibility.
