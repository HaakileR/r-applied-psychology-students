# Teaching Notes

## Why synthetic data?

Synthetic data allow students to practice realistic analysis without ethical concerns related to privacy, consent, or sensitive participant information.

## Why use psychology-style variables?

Students learn faster when examples are close to their field. Variables such as stress, motivation, digital stress, well-being, and exam anxiety are familiar enough to support interpretation.

## Suggested classroom discussion questions

1. Why should raw data remain unchanged?
2. What is the difference between an item and a scale score?
3. Why do some items need reverse scoring?
4. What does Cronbach's alpha tell us?
5. Why should we visualize data before running models?
6. What is the difference between statistical significance and practical importance?
7. How can R support reproducible research?

## Common student difficulties

- forgetting to set the working directory;
- running scripts from the wrong folder;
- confusing item scores with scale scores;
- forgetting reverse scoring;
- interpreting p-values mechanically;
- not saving outputs in organized folders.

## Instructor recommendation

Ask students to keep a personal analysis log. It can include:

- what script they ran;
- what error they received;
- how they solved the error;
- what result they obtained;
- how they interpret the result.
